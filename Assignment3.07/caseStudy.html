Case Study: Telehealth



Recap

Telehealth wants to make healthcare more accessible by breaking down barriers related to cost, travel, etc.

Explantion

Cost, time, and travel are all factors that deter people from seeking medical treatment. Telehealth is especially useful in rural areas.

Most Effective

Telehealth offers an online solution for doctors appointments. Unlike their physical counterparts, online check-ups quick, easy, and undisruptive, making people less reluctant to schedule an appointment.

Explanation

Around 26.4% of people making under $25,000 a year say they've used a Telehealth video call in the past four weeks (source). This shows how effective it is because more low-income people are using healthcare services.

Use of Tech

Telehealth offers direct access to health care providers over the phone or on video.

Explanation

The only requirement for this service is internet connection. Doctors are able to collect data from devices used by the patient and still see the patient's symptoms in real time. The experience is almost the same as one would be in person.

Implications

Online options are extremely viable, and can combat barriers often posed by poverty, such as living in a rural area or high cost.

Explanation

It can cost a lot to travel to a hospital frequently for visits and a lot of time as well. For low-income people, those are valuable resources. One way to avoid this is by having doctor's appointments online. This makes people more willing to seek medical help if they don't need to devote a lot of time, money, and effort for a simple check-up.
